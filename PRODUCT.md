# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site serves businesses, individuals, and entrepreneurs who need signage, business cards, flyers, brochures, stickers, banners, print materials, graphic design support, or related installation services. Most visitors are trying to quickly understand whether The Point can produce the thing they need and then start a conversation through WhatsApp or the contact form.

## Product Purpose

The Point website is a small brochure site for a Liberia, Guanacaste printshop and signage studio. It should work as an additional communication channel, introduce the company, show the main service families, present representative products, and convert interest into a direct inquiry.

Success means a visitor can answer three questions fast:

- What does The Point make?
- Can I see proof of the type of work they do?
- How do I contact them now?

## Brand Personality

Direct, practical, graphic.

The brand should feel like a printshop with real production capability: bold color, high contrast, confident service language, and enough polish to feel professional without becoming corporate or generic.

## Anti-references

Avoid a generic SaaS landing page, soft pastel startup visuals, over-designed agency theatrics, heavy glassmorphism, stock-photo brochure sameness, and a redesign that erases the existing CMY-on-black identity.

Avoid changing the site into an ecommerce catalog. Product blocks are lead-generation prompts, not checkout cards.

## Design Principles

- Preserve the printshop identity. Black, white, cyan, magenta, and yellow are not decoration here; they are the visual memory of the brand.
- Show proof quickly. Use real work photography and product imagery before abstract explanation.
- Keep the path to contact short. WhatsApp and the project form should be easy to find on every meaningful page.
- Polish without replacing. Improve spacing, responsive behavior, accessibility, SEO, and form behavior while keeping the original visual direction recognizable.
- Write for action. Copy should be brief, concrete, and service-oriented.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target practical WCAG AA improvements for contrast, focus visibility, keyboard navigation, reduced motion, form labels, and responsive layout. Motion should never hide content or block access. Forms should work with and without JavaScript.
